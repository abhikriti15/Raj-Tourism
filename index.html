The City of a Hundred Islands

About Banswara

Banswara, located in the southern part of Rajasthan, is known for its picturesque landscapes, lakes, and rich tribal culture. With over a hundred islands formed by the Mahi River, Banswara has earned the title "City of a Hundred Islands." It is a haven for nature lovers, offering serene spots like lakes, forests, and waterfalls, along with temples and tribal traditions.

Top Destinations in Banswara

Mahi Dam

Built on the Mahi River, Mahi Dam is a stunning spot surrounded by lush greenery. The dam is a popular spot for locals and tourists to enjoy picnics and scenic views.

Anand Sagar Lake

Also known as Bai Talab, Anand Sagar Lake is a beautiful, artificial lake. Surrounded by holy trees and temples, it is a peaceful spot for nature lovers and devotees alike.

Tripura Sundari Temple

One of the Shakti Peethas in Rajasthan, this ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari is a major pilgrimage site, attracting thousands of devotees each year.

Kagdi Pick Up Weir

Located on the Udaipur-Banswara road, Kagdi Pick Up Weir is a serene spot with beautiful gardens and fountains, perfect for family outings and nature photography.
